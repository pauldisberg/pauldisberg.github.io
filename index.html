Exploring Dark Matter Halos and Galactic Dynamics

I'm a PhD student in astrophysics investigating dark matter halos and galactic dynamics through simulations and observations. My current work focuses on dark matter substructure, strong gravitational lensing, and integral-field spectroscopy, combining Bayesian inference and machine learning. My academic goals are to publish rigorous, open, and reproducible research, collaborate across disciplines, and complete my dissertation advancing our understanding of galaxy formation.

About Me

I am a PhD student in astrophysics at the Institute for Cosmic Research, working with multi-wavelength datasets and cosmological simulations. My research explores how dark matter halos influence the observable structures of galaxies, leveraging Bayesian inference, machine learning, and high-performance computing.

Before my PhD, I completed an MSc in Physics with a thesis on weak gravitational lensing mass maps. I enjoy building tools for reproducible science, contributing to open-source projects, and mentoring undergraduate students in data analysis and scientific communication.

Subhalo Populations in Milky Way–mass Halos

Quantifying subhalo mass functions and tidal disruption using high-resolution N-body simulations to compare with satellite galaxy observations.

Strong Lensing Constraints on Halo Concentrations

Using ensemble lens models to map the distribution of halo concentrations and test predictions from ΛCDM alongside baryonic feedback effects.

ArXiv Preprint Poster (PDF)

Resolved Kinematics of Dwarf Galaxies
