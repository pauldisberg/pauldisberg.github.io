ASTROPHYSICS PHD CANDIDATE

Paul Disberg

I am an astrophysicist who studies the dynamics of kicked neutron stars, both isolated and in binary systems. By comparing theoretical models with observations, my work aims to deepen our understanding of supernova kicks, binary evolution, and the high-energy signals produced by neutron star mergers.

About

Paul is a Dutch astrophysicist currently pursuing his PhD at Monash University in Melbourne under the supervision of Professor Ilya Mandel. He completed his Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees in Physics and Astronomy at Radboud University in the Netherlands, where he also earned degrees in Philosophy with a focus on the philosophy of science as it relates to astrophysics. His current research centers on neutron star kicks and their effects on binary systems, and he has also published on topics including gamma-ray bursts and binary black hole masses.
